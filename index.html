Here's a time line of Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931- Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, also known as Dr A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on October 15 at Rameshwaram in Tamil Nadu. His incredible journey from hardships to People's President set an example for everyone to not give up at any circumstances and we should not allow the problem to defeat us.
  • Kalam hailed from a financially poor background, where to support his family income he used to sell newspapers after the school hours. His father Jainulabdeen was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque. While his mother Ashiamma was a housewife.
  • Kalam completed his schooling form the Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram. Later, he went to Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, then affiliated with the University of Madras, from where he graduated in physics in 1954.
  • 1955- He moved to Madras to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology.
  • After graduation he joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (by Press Information Bureau, Government of India) as a scientist after becoming a member of the Defence Research & Development Service (DRDS).
  • Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1969.
  • 1970-1990-Kalam played a vital role to develop the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects. After this he showcased his excellence at the time of Pokhran-II nuclear tests.
  • 1992- He served as chief scientific advisor to the prime minister
  • 2002- He contested for the country's top post against Lakshmi Sehgal and interestingly he was backed by both the Bharatiya Janata Party and the Indian National Congress, the two leading political parties of India.
  • Awards-He was conferred with prestigious Padma Bhushan (1981), the Padma Vibhushan (1990), and Bharat Ratna (1997). In 2002, he was elected as the 11th President of India and served the term till 2007.
  • After serving a term as the President, he returned to what he always liked 'academics'. Tragically, Dr Kalam took his last breath while delivering a lecture at the IIM Shillong. In 2010, the United Nations declared his birth anniversary as 'World Student's Day'.
  • For his brilliance in scientific field , Kalam attained the title of " Missile Man of India". He also wrote books such as Inspiring Thoughts, Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power Within India, The Luminous Sparks, Wings of Fire: An Autobiography and so on. His contributions to the country will always be remembered and appreciated.
